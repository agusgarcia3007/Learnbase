How many hours did you waste this week uploading videos one by one? Writing descriptions nobody reads? Setting up quizzes that take longer than recording the actual content?

If you create online courses, probably too many.

Most online educators take 4 to 6 weeks to launch a course. The worst part: most of that time goes into repetitive tasks that have nothing to do with teaching.

## The real problem with traditional platforms

Classic LMS platforms were built for system administrators, not creators. Every video requires filling out endless forms. Every module needs manual configuration. By the time you're done, you're so exhausted that your next course idea stays in the "someday" drawer.

<Callout type="warn">
60% of creators abandon their online academy project before publishing their first course. The main reason isn't lack of expertise or audience. It's technical burnout.
</Callout>

## What changes with AI

Artificial intelligence applied to online education automates the tedious parts:

<Steps>
<Step>
### Automatic course structure
Describe your topic in a paragraph and get a complete outline. Modules, lessons, logical order. You can adjust it later, but you have a solid foundation in minutes instead of days.
</Step>

<Step>
### Resource generation
AI analyzes your videos and generates summaries, key points, and quiz questions. Work that used to take hours per lesson now gets done while you grab coffee.
</Step>

<Step>
### Smart search for students
Your students type "how do I configure the API?" and the AI takes them to the exact minute where you explain that. No more "it's in lesson 4, I think, or maybe 7".
</Step>
</Steps>

## Tutors that work while you sleep

This already exists. And it completely changes what you can offer as an educator.
